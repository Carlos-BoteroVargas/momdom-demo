I have reviewed the landing page and here is my feedback.

There’s a lot here that I genuinely love:
• the emotional tone
• the structure
• the softness
• the clarity of the product direction
• the balloon ecosystem concept
• the overall feeling that this is becoming something substantial and scalable

As I went through everything carefully, I realized the biggest thing I want to protect moving forward is the emotional identity of MomDom.

I want the experience to feel:
• relieving
• emotionally intelligent
• calming
• supportive
• quietly powerful

—not overly corporate, productivity-app, or aggressive SaaS startup feeling.

I think the strongest direction is:
emotional softness + intelligent systems together.

Below are my section-by-section notes, positioning adjustments, and updated wording direction.

Overall Direction

The updated site is significantly stronger structurally and does a much better job communicating that MomDom is a real ecosystem/platform rather than just a concept.

The original blue-background version felt:
• immersive
• calming
• emotionally relieving
• soft
• emotionally distinct

The newer version is stronger from a usability and product perspective, but some sections are starting to feel slightly too:
• corporate
• productivity-focused
• SaaS/app-platform oriented

I want the overall experience to feel:
“finally, something that understands how my brain feels.”

—not:
“here’s another organization app.”

The experience should feel like relief first and organization second.

I’d like to simplify and tighten this section so it feels more focused, emotionally clear, and aligned with the actual MVP1 structure.

Right now I think having 8 balloons starts making the section feel slightly too expansive and feature-heavy this early on.

I’d like to reduce this section down to 6 core categories:
• Mom’s Mental Tabs
• Kids
• Health
• Meals
• Household
• Planning

I think this will:
• create more clarity
• reduce visual overwhelm
• strengthen emotional focus
• improve mobile readability
